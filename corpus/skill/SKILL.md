# Alex Hormozi Money Models

Source note: this skill is grounded in `../extractions/money-models-course.md`, official Acquisition.com Money Models course navigation, and local Money Models lesson transcripts. Do not add money model rules unless they are verified against those transcripts or another canonical Hormozi source.

## Workflow

1. Identify the current acquisition path.
2. Calculate CAC, gross profit, and payback period.
3. Check whether lifetime gross profit exceeds CAC by enough to sustain the business.
4. Check whether 30-day gross profit can cover CAC.
5. Map current offers into attraction, upsell, downsell, and continuity.
6. Find the simplest offer-layer change that improves CAC, GP, or payback.
7. Avoid adding operational drag unless the economics justify it.

## Core Principle

Lifetime gross profit must exceed CAC.

Use gross profit, not revenue. Revenue can look attractive while gross profit cannot support acquisition, overhead, or scale.

Minimum viability check:

```text
LTGP / CAC >= 3
```

Treat 3:1 as a floor, not the goal.

## Three Numbers That Matter

### CAC

CAC is the total cost required to sell a new customer.

Include:

- ad spend
- acquisition payroll
- creative costs
- software
- sales commissions
- sales and marketing salaries
- acquisition team costs

Formula:

```text
CAC = Total acquisition costs / New customers acquired
```

Track both media CAC and fully loaded CAC when useful, but diagnose from fully loaded CAC.

### Gross Profit

Gross profit is what remains after the cost of delivering the product or service.

Formula:

```text
Gross Profit = Price - Cost of Goods Sold
Gross Margin = Gross Profit / Price
```

Gross profit is not net profit. Rent, admin, utilities, and general overhead are not COGS unless they rise with each unit or customer delivered.

For service businesses, use 80% gross margin as the working rule of thumb unless the business model justifies a different target.

### Payback Period

Payback period is how long it takes gross profit from a customer to recover CAC.

Shorter payback creates faster growth because cash recycles sooner.

## Client-Financed Acquisition

Use three levels:

1. **Level 1**: lifetime gross profit is greater than CAC.
2. **Level 2**: 30-day gross profit is greater than CAC.
3. **Level 3**: 30-day gross profit is greater than 2x CAC.

Level 2 means the business can recover acquisition spend inside a typical 30-day credit cycle. Level 3 means one customer funds their own acquisition and another customer's acquisition.

## Four Offer Types

Map the money model into four offer categories:

1. **Attraction offers**: liquidate CAC and turn strangers into customers.
2. **Upsell offers**: maximize profit after the first yes.
3. **Downsell offers**: maximize conversion when people say no.
4. **Continuity offers**: stabilize cash flow and keep customers buying.

Do not assume every business needs all four immediately. Start with as few offers as possible and add layers only when they improve the economics enough to justify operational complexity.

## Improving Gross Profit: More Better New

When improving gross profit without changing the core cost structure:

- **More**: sell more quantity, duration, or frequency.
- **Better**: sell a higher-quality or premium version.
- **New**: sell a complementary product or service.

## Upsell Logic

An upsell is whatever is offered next.

Good upsells:

- solve the next problem revealed by the first offer
- happen when the buyer can see the next problem
- are believable from this seller
- increase profit per customer

Upsells fail when they are unwanted, mistimed, unrelated, or not believed.

## Diagnostic Questions

- What is fully loaded CAC?
- What is gross profit per customer?
- What is gross margin?
- How long is payback period?
- Is LTGP at least 3x CAC?
- Does 30-day gross profit cover CAC?
- Which offer type is missing or weak?
- Can we improve economics with one layer before adding many offers?
- Are we increasing profit per customer or only adding complexity?

## Quality Checklist

- Calculations use gross profit, not revenue.
- CAC includes acquisition labor and tools, not just ad spend.
- Payback period is explicit.
- Offer categories are mapped clearly.
- The recommended change targets CAC, GP, or payback.
- The model stays as simple as possible.
- New offers solve real next problems for customers.
