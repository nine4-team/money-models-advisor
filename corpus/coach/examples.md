## E1 ‚Äî Gym Launch (Hormozi's flagship case)

- **Business shape:** fitness / service / local / recurring
- **Situation:** Facebook 5X'd gym lead costs; standard "low barrier offer" model (LBO = $29 trial ‚Üí 35% convert ‚Üí $600 GP / 10 walk-ins) couldn't keep up.
- **Money model applied:** sell-to-goal attraction ($2K / $500 tiers) ‚Üí classic upsell on meals + supplements (~$85 GP/person on supplements alone) ‚Üí continuity via annual commitment ‚Üí prepayment discount.
- **Outcome:** 30-day GP went from $600 to $9,644 per 10 walk-ins. 16√ó improvement. Licensed to 5,000+ gyms, 4:1 first-30-day ROAS benchmark.
- **Key numbers:** $100 CAC, $100 GP from supplements alone = offset first-sale CAC entirely.
- **Tags:** `#service-business` `#high-ticket` `#recurring-revenue` `#transformation-based` `#flagship-case`
- **Source:** `¬ß2.2.c`, `¬ß1.1.c-d`

## E2 ‚Äî The Meals Company Failure

- **Business shape:** meals / DTC / recurring / low-margin
- **Situation:** $100 CAC, $500 lifetime revenue looked great. Team wanted to spend more.
- **What was broken:** 20% gross margin. LTGP = $100. LTGP/CAC = 1:1. Would need $1,500 LTGP to justify.
- **Outcome:** Hormozi shut it down rather than fix it ‚Äî 80% of complaints came from meals, only 5% of profits.
- **Lesson:** always think in gross profit, never revenue. The fix would have been price, COGS, or scope ‚Äî not a bolt-on money model.
- **Tags:** `#low-margin` `#cautionary-tale` `#unit-economics` `#diagnostic`
- **Source:** `¬ß1.2.b`

## E7 ‚Äî Rental Car Counter (9 Sequential Upsells)

## E8 ‚Äî Guarantee-Removal Feature Downsell (3X Close Rate)

- **Business shape:** high-ticket services
- **Situation:** Friend had 25% close rate on $4,000 offer.
- **Mechanic:** When customer balked, removed the guarantee and offered same product at $2,000. Many re-upsold themselves to the $4,000 version with guarantee once they saw the value tradeoff. 25% close ‚Üí 75% close (35% at $4K + 40% at $2K).
- **Lesson:** Remove the guarantee first. Highest re-upsell rate of any feature.
- **Tags:** `#feature-downsell` `#guarantee` `#high-ticket` `#re-upsell`
- **Source:** `¬ß5.4.a`

## E9 ‚Äî The HR Platform Rebate ("Trial with Penalty" Origin)

- **Business shape:** B2B SaaS
- **Situation:** Hormozi's wife Leila evaluated several HR platforms, all roughly equivalent.
- **Mechanic:** Winning platform charged $500, rebated if she attended onboarding/training sessions. She attended, learned their software, never switched.

## E17 ‚Äî Biweekly Billing Bonus

- **Business shape:** any subscription
- **Mechanic:** Bill every 2 weeks aligned to paychecks. ~3 collections in the first 30 days vs. 1 on monthly cycles. Far fewer declined payments.

## E20 ‚Äî Low-Price, Under-Consuming Customer LTV Hack

- **Business shape:** service portfolio
- **Situation:** Second-highest LTV segment in a portfolio company was a low-mid price tier.
- **Mechanic:** Team proactively reached out to under-consumers: "You're using 4 of 6 features ‚Äî let me lower you to this rate." Customers perceived it as ethical. Churn collapsed.
- **Lesson:** Lower price on under-consumers before they churn. You trade short-term revenue for long-term LTV.
- **Tags:** `#feature-downsell` `#retention` `#proactive-outreach` `#ltv`
