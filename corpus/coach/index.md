## Chapter 3 — Attraction Offers

**When to use:** the constraint is getting customers in the door at all, or getting them in cheaply enough that the rest of the model can work. Bring strangers to first purchase. Objective = liquidate CAC.
**Tags:** `#attraction` `#lead-generation` `#first-sale`

### §3.1 Attraction Offers Overview — Liquidate CAC
source: `transcripts/attraction-offers.txt`
**When to use:** orienting to what "attraction offer" means before picking a type.
**Key ideas:** objective = liquidate or exceed CAC, attraction sits at the top of the funnel, six specific types follow.
**Tags:** `#attraction` `#cac` `#overview`

### §3.2 Win Your Money Back — Goal-based refund with before/after
source: `transcripts/win-your-money-back.txt`
**When to use:** transformation-based business (fitness, weight loss, coaching, rehab), high-accountability desired, need before/after proof for marketing, reverse risk for the buyer.
**Key ideas:** customer pays upfront, earns refund (or store credit) by hitting a goal, before/after becomes marketing asset worth more than any refund paid, works only where transformation is measurable.
**Tags:** `#attraction` `#risk-reversal` `#transformation-based` `#high-ticket` `#service-business` `#accountability`

### §3.3 Free Giveaways — Grand prize + participation trophy
source: `transcripts/free-giveaways.txt`
**When to use:** need large lead volume fast, have a high-value signature offer you can showcase, want qualification data from applicants.
**Key ideas:** advertise grand prize, give partial scholarship/promotional offer to everyone else, application questions double as sales ammo, dual prizes 2-4× referrals, cash giveaways are worst.
**Tags:** `#attraction` `#lead-generation` `#urgency` `#social-proof` `#referral` `#price-anchor`
- §3.3.a How I learned it — scholarship story
- §3.3.b Structure — grand prize + participation trophy
- §3.3.c Execution timeline — 3-7 day countdown, daily value
- §3.3.d Promotional offer math — 10-30% of gross margin
- §3.3.e Advanced tactics — dual prizes, back-pocket offer, recurring setup

### §3.4 Decoy Offers — Advertise cheap, present premium
source: `transcripts/decoy-offers.txt`
**When to use:** the real product is premium but ads for it don't pull, customers need to be in the conversation before they'll commit, target 70-80% choosing premium once both are shown.
**Key ideas:** advertise the stripped-down decoy, present both side-by-side, sorting question ("free stuff or lasting results?"), if <70% take premium the contrast isn't sharp enough.
**Tags:** `#attraction` `#decoy` `#price-anchor` `#high-ticket` `#sales-process`

### §3.5 Buy X Get Y Free — "Buy 1, get 2 free" beats "66% off"
source: `transcripts/buy-x-get-y.txt`
**When to use:** recurring product/service, want prepaid cash upfront, want a discount that feels bigger than its economics, scaling bulk/duration commitments.
**Key ideas:** identical economics to % off but better psychology, give more free than paid (buy 6 get 12 > buy 12 get 6), cap prepayments at 10% of customer base, spread discount across term.
**Tags:** `#attraction` `#prepayment` `#bundling` `#discount` `#recurring-revenue` `#cash-flow`

### §3.6 Pay Less Now / Pay More Later — $0 down vs discounted upfront
source: `transcripts/pay-less-now.txt`
**When to use:** physical products or one-time services, customer balks at full price, want to reverse risk and give the customer agency.
**Key ideas:** pay-later option ($0 down, full price after delivery, cancel if unsatisfied), pay-now option (33-66% discount + bonuses), target ~10% cancellation, advertise as "free" since $0 down.
**Tags:** `#attraction` `#risk-reversal` `#product-business` `#pay-later` `#discount`

### §3.7 Free with Consumption — Trust before sale via content/experience
source: `transcripts/free-with-consumption.txt`
**When to use:** high-ticket offers (typically $500+), customer needs to experience value before paying, belief-breaking and education are prerequisite to sale.
**Key ideas:** free consumption → trust → expensive-offer acceptance, time required scales with price ($20 = 7 sec ad; $5K+ = major content), 5-day challenge structure, three beliefs to break (circumstances / other people / self-doubt), ~10% virtual webinar conversion.
**Tags:** `#attraction` `#trust-building` `#high-ticket` `#content-marketing` `#belief-breaking` `#digital-product`

## Chapter 4 — Upsell Offers

**When to use:** constraint is maximizing gross profit per customer. Customers will buy, but aren't worth enough. Most real-world profit often hides here (fries-and-Coke beat the burger).
**Tags:** `#upsell` `#maximize-profit` `#post-purchase`

### §4.1 Upsell Offers Overview — Maximize profit per customer
source: `transcripts/upsell-offers.txt`
**When to use:** orienting to upsell logic before picking a mechanic.
**Key ideas:** upsells are made after initial sale, burger-shop math (fries/soda/supersize = 8.5× GP), often majority of profit despite smaller transaction counts.
**Tags:** `#upsell` `#ltv` `#overview`

### §4.2 Classic Upsell — "You can't have X without Y"
source: `transcripts/classic-upsell.txt`
**When to use:** attraction offer delivers something that obviously requires accessories or complements (storage → locks, gym → supplements, PT → orthotics), want 80%+ uptake like McDonald's fries.
**Key ideas:** free or cheap core + essential accompaniment, no-sell sale ("you don't want anything else, do you?"), upsell should feel required for results, 80% benchmark.
**Tags:** `#upsell` `#classic-upsell` `#bundling` `#service-business` `#product-business` `#accessory`
- §4.2.a How I learned it — fur coat storage + no-sell sale
- §4.2.b Description — "you can't have X without Y"
- §4.2.c Storage unit example — free month at $127
- §4.2.d Applied examples — weight loss, PT, education
- §4.2.e Important points + 80% close rate benchmark

### §4.3 Menu Upsell — Unsell, prescribe, ask preference, card on file
source: `transcripts/menu-upsell.txt`
**When to use:** supplement/accessory/add-on menus at a consult, want high-trust sales flow, want frictionless close via card on file.
**Key ideas:** 4-step framework (Unsell → Prescribe → Ask Preference → Card on File), crossing out non-applicable products builds trust, "vanilla or chocolate" (both = yes), card-on-file kills friction.
**Tags:** `#upsell` `#sales-process` `#trust-building` `#service-business`

### §4.4 Anchor Upsell — Premium first, main offer feels like a bargain
source: `transcripts/anchor-upsell.txt`
**When to use:** presenting options face-to-face or on a sales call, have a legitimate premium tier worth 5-10× main price, main offer feels high in isolation.
**Key ideas:** present anchor at 5-10× price, wait for the gasp, ask about secondary features, then present main offer, only secondary features vary between tiers, 1-in-10 buying the anchor doubles average revenue.
**Tags:** `#upsell` `#price-anchor` `#high-ticket` `#sales-process`

### §4.5 Rollover Upsell — Credit a prior purchase toward a bigger one
source: `transcripts/rollover-upsell.txt`
**When to use:** winning back old customers, rescuing upset customers, stealing competitors' customers, upselling current customers to higher tier.
**Key ideas:** 4 situations (win-back, rescue, competitor-steal, current-customer), upsell must be 4-5× rollover credit, "I owe you money" email, gift card hack for paid referrals.
**Tags:** `#upsell` `#win-back` `#referral` `#competitor-steal` `#retention`

## Chapter 5 — Downsell Offers

**When to use:** customers say no to the primary offer. Constraint is conversion. Downsells must remove value proportionally when lowering price — never just discount without changing what they get.
**Tags:** `#downsell` `#objection-handling` `#maximize-conversion`

### §5.1 Downsells Overview — Maximize conversion, never just drop price
source: `transcripts/downsells.txt`
**When to use:** orienting to downsell logic.
**Key ideas:** never lower price without changing what they get, downsells convert "no" into "yes," alternatives must be proportional in value.
**Tags:** `#downsell` `#overview`

### §5.2 Payment Plans — Spread pain, not price
source: `transcripts/payment-plans.txt`
**When to use:** customer wants the offer but can't afford it today (not overall), want to avoid discounting, want billing aligned to paycheck cycles.
**Key ideas:** people reject TODAY-cost not total-cost, 7-step flow, anchor with payment-plan price first then show prepay discount, third-party financing adds 35% revenue, biweekly billing aligned to paychecks (~3 collections in first 30 days, fewer declines), pay commission on cash collected today.
**Tags:** `#downsell` `#payment-plan` `#cash-flow` `#objection-handling` `#financing`

### §5.3 Free Trials (Trial with Penalty) — Free if you meet terms
source: `transcripts/free-trials.txt`
**When to use:** recurring products/services, customer must do work to get results, customer already said no and you want a structured last chance.
**Key ideas:** free if they meet terms, fees for non-consumption (not "penalty" language), always get card upfront, sell staying and paying before explaining fees, 7-step process, upsell paths for "like it / hate it / didn't use it."
**Tags:** `#downsell` `#risk-reversal` `#service-business` `#recurring-revenue` `#objection-handling` `#trial`
- §5.3.a How I learned it — HR platform rebate story
- §5.3.b Trial with penalty — definition and logic
- §5.3.c Examples — B2C, B2B, therapy, fee weighting
- §5.3.d The 7-step sales process
- §5.3.e Upselling from a trial — like/hate/don't-use scenarios
- §5.3.f Tweaking, naming, vs pay-less-now

### §5.4 Feature Downsells — Change what they get, not just the price
source: `transcripts/feature-downsells.txt`
**When to use:** customer rejects price, all other downsells exhausted, you can legitimately remove value (quantity, quality, service dimensions, the guarantee itself).
**Key ideas:** remove features to force re-up-sell, first pass = valuable feature + tiny price drop (usually the guarantee), 14 service-quality dimensions to adjust, barter for advertising is the only legitimate way to lower price without changing product, LTV-extension pro tip (proactively lower price for under-consuming customers).
**Tags:** `#downsell` `#feature-removal` `#guarantee` `#service-business` `#barter` `#retention`
- §5.4.a How I learned it — guarantee-removal 3X close rate
- §5.4.b Description — lower price by changing the package
- §5.4.c 14 service-quality dimensions
- §5.4.d Process — valuable feature / small drop, then invert
- §5.4.e Barter-for-advertising — the only legitimate price cut

## Chapter 6 — Continuity Offers

**When to use:** constraint is retention, cash flow stability, or enterprise value at exit. Customers buy once and leave. Recurring revenue is worth 2-4× more at sale than transactional revenue.
**Tags:** `#continuity` `#recurring-revenue` `#retention` `#enterprise-value`

### §6.1 Continuity Offers Overview — Why recurring revenue matters
source: `transcripts/continuity-offers.txt`
**When to use:** motivating continuity, calculating LTV impact of churn reduction.
**Key ideas:** lifetime revenue = price ÷ monthly churn rate, 10% → 3% churn = 233% LTV lift, recurring biz sells for 2-4× transactional multiple, "big head, long tail" (upfront + ongoing creates sunk-cost that cuts churn).
**Tags:** `#continuity` `#churn` `#ltv` `#enterprise-value` `#overview`

### §6.2 Continuity Bonus Offers — Advertise the bonus, not the subscription
source: `transcripts/continuity-bonus.txt`
**When to use:** converting transactional customers to recurring, make the subscription feel free via bonus value, justify a standalone-vs-continuity price premium.
**Key ideas:** bonus should exceed first month's value, standalone-vs-continuity pricing data (1.33× → 50% take continuity; 2.66× → 90%), people pay ~33% more to avoid subscriptions, status/title bonuses are cheapest/highest-perceived-value.
**Tags:** `#continuity` `#bonus` `#status` `#conversion` `#prepayment`

### §6.3 Continuity Discount Offers — Time-based discounts for commitment
source: `transcripts/continuity-discounts.txt`
**When to use:** churn spikes at a known month, want to lock in long-term commitment, reduce involuntary churn from declined cards.
**Key ideas:** 4 discount applications (front-loaded / back-loaded / spread / gift-card), lifetime discount kicks in at known highest-churn month, collect card AND ACH ("3% fee waived"), make cancellation EASY but capture a feedback-for-fee-waive exit interview.
**Tags:** `#continuity` `#discount` `#retention` `#churn` `#cancellation`

### §6.4 Waived Fee Offers — Startup fee you waive for commitment
source: `transcripts/waived-fee.txt`
**When to use:** high-commitment service (SaaS, coaching, fitness membership), want sunk-cost psychology, punish early cancellation without explicit lock-in.
**Key ideas:** month-to-month pays setup fee OR committed pays nothing-upfront-but-owes-fee-if-cancel, startup fee = 3-5× monthly rate, creative fee names, cancellation-fee-for-a-cause tactic, <5% early cancellation = target.
**Tags:** `#continuity` `#commitment` `#sunk-cost` `#service-business` `#high-ticket`
