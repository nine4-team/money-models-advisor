So let me talk about the downsell. A lot of people try to do a downsell and they do it in a way that actually insults their customer. They show the exact same product, but they say, "Well, okay. So you don't want the $97 version. How about the $47 version?" And the customer's like, "Wait, these look like the same thing. Why are you showing me the same thing but charging me less?" It feels insulting. It feels like you were ripping me off before and now you're trying to scam me with a cheaper version. That's not a downsell. Here's what a downsell is. A downsell is when you take out one or more features from a product and you reduce the price proportionally. So if you go from a five-module product to a three-module product, you might go from $97 to $57. So the customer sees that you actually removed value. They understand the price reduction. Okay, so let's talk about some really good downsell examples. The first example is payment plans. So a customer comes to your funnel and you say, "Okay, here's the price. It's $297." And they say, "Nope. I don't have that right now." So what you could do is you could say, "Okay, how about instead of paying 297 dollars all upfront, you pay three payments of $99?" Now, you could do four payments of $80 each. You could do two payments of $160 each. Whatever you want. But the key is you're giving them an alternative that's more affordable right now, but they still get the full product. And that's a downsell. Another type of downsell is a feature downsell. So let's say you have a course that comes with personalized coaching. And you say, "Okay, it's $297, and that includes five coaching calls with me." They say, "Nope, that's too expensive." So you say, "Okay, how about I remove the coaching and you just get the course for $97?" Now they get less features, but the product is more affordable. That's a downsell. Another one is a trial with a penalty. So you might say, "Okay, you can use this for 30 days risk-free. But in order to qualify for this trial, you have to give me your credit card." So you're reducing the risk for the customer by letting them try it, but you're adding a small commitment hurdle to qualify for that trial. So those are a few different types of downsells that actually work. Let me give you a rule for downsells that will help you make better downsells in your own funnel. The rule is this: A downsell works best when the customer is saying no because of price, not because of the product. So if they're saying no because they don't like your product, a downsell won't help. But if they're saying no because they think it's too expensive, then a downsell is perfect because you're making it more affordable. So you gotta make sure you're using downsells in the right situation.
