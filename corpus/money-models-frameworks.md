## 1. Foundation: How Businesses Make Money

### Core Principle
Lifetime Gross Profit (LTGP) must exceed Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) — ideally by at least 3:1.

**Formula:** `LTGP / CAC ≥ 3.0` (minimum for viable business)

### The Meals Company Cautionary Tale
- $100 CAC, $500 lifetime revenue — looked great on paper
- But 20% gross margin → only $100 lifetime gross profit
- $100 LTGP vs $100 CAC = 1:1 ratio = unprofitable after overhead
- Needed $1,500 in LTGP (not $500 in revenue) to make sense
- Lesson: Always think in gross profit, never revenue

### The Competitive Advantage Insight
Two businesses selling the same number of units per month:
- Business A: $17M profit/year
- Business B: $300K profit/year
- Difference: gross profit per customer (56X difference)
- "He who makes his customer worth more to his business than to his competition wins"

## 2. The Three Numbers That Matter

These three metrics triangulate acquisition — getting new customers profitably and quickly.

### 2a. CAC — Cost to Acquire a Customer

**Definition:** Total cost to sell one new customer, including all advertising, payroll, software, creative, and sales commissions.

**Formula:** `Total Acquisition Costs / Number of New Customers = CAC`

**Example:** ($3,000 emailer + $200 software + $800 commissions) / 8 sales = $500 CAC

**Two Types:**
- **Media CAC:** Just ad spend per customer
- **Fully Loaded CAC:** All costs including salaries, tools, commissions

**Ways to Improve CAC:**
1. Make better offers (higher response rates, higher conversion)
2. Use the free vs. discount continuum (free generates 9X more leads — Dan Ariely's Hershey Kiss experiment)
3. Create flow first, then monetize, then add friction

**The Free vs. Discount Continuum:**
- 100% off (free) = highest demand
- 0% off (full price) = lowest demand
- Best approach: either fully free or full price — moderate discounts (10-20%) rarely change behavior
- Use "insane" discounts (50%+) if discounting at all

**Five Ways to Display a Discount:**
1. Percentage off (87% off)
2. Absolute amount off ($87 off)
3. Value equivalency (save a steak dinner)
4. Portion off (4 months free on a 12-month plan)
5. Price comparison ($800 vs. ~~$1,200~~)

**Myth Debunked — "Free Brings Broke People":**
Tested across 20 gym locations: free vs. paid generated the same closing percentage. The only difference was lead cost. Wealthy people want free things too.

**Adding Friction to Free Offers (without making them worse):**
1. Qualification questions
2. Number of steps in the process
3. Targeting requirements
4. Require consumption before next step
5. Increase ad length

**The Flow-Monetize-Friction Framework:**
1. **Create flow** — big, attractive free offer to generate volume
2. **Monetize** — convert leads into paying customers
3. **Add friction** — optimize and filter to improve quality

### 2b. Gross Profit (GP)

**Definition:** Price minus Cost of Goods Sold (COGS). What remains after delivering the service or product.

**Formula:** `Price - COGS = Gross Profit`
**Margin:** `Gross Profit / Price × 100 = Gross Margin %`

**Rule of Thumb for Service Businesses:** 80% minimum gross margin. If delivery costs $100, sell for $500+.

**Three Ways to Increase Gross Profit:**
1. **More** — sell more quantity or longer duration (buy in bulk, buy again)
2. **Better** — sell higher-quality versions (organic vs. regular, premium vs. standard)
3. **New** — sell complementary products (burgers → fries and Coke)

**Key Insight:** You can only drive costs down to zero, but you can drive lifetime value infinitely high. Focus on increasing value, not reducing costs.

### 2c. Payback Period (PPD)

**Definition:** How long until gross profit from a customer equals the CAC — the break-even point.

**Formula:** `(CAC - First Month Gross Profit) / Monthly Recurring Gross Profit = Remaining Months to Payback`

**Example:** CAC = $160, Monthly GP = $80
- Month 1: $80 GP → still $80 deficit
- Month 2: $80 GP → break even at 2 months

**Critical:** Always subtract COGS from revenue when calculating. Many businesses forget this.

**Tactics to Shorten Payback Period:**
1. Make more offers in first 30 days
2. Add first/last month payment upfront
3. Add initiation, onboarding, enrollment, or activation fees
4. Example: $160 CAC, $80 GP/month → add $80 enrollment fee → break even Month 1

### Five Critical Moments to Make Upsells (Within First 30 Days)

1. **Immediately after first sale** — if it naturally solves the next problem
2. **Next step (24-72 hours)** — when a new problem is discovered
3. **After a big win/milestone** — when they experience success
4. **Halfway point** — historically high receptivity
5. **Last chance** — end of service period (fallback, not primary)

**Golden Rule:** "Sell at the point of greatest deprivation, not greatest satisfaction." After a customer accomplishes a goal, new problems emerge — that's when they're most receptive.

## 3. Client-Financed Acquisition (CFA)

**Definition:** A process in which gross profit generated from a single client within the first 30 days exceeds the cost of acquiring that customer.

### Three Levels of Advertising

| Level | Condition | Outcome |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Level 1** | Lifetime GP > CAC (≥3X) | Viable but slow growth; need outside capital to scale |
| **Level 2** | 30-Day GP > CAC | Break even in one month; use credit cards to finance growth |
| **Level 3** | 30-Day GP > 2X CAC | Exponential growth; one customer pays for themselves + another |

### Level 3 Math — Exponential Growth
With a 2X multiplier (each customer funds themselves + one more):
- Month 1: 1 customer
- Month 2: 2 customers
- Month 3: 4 customers
- Month 4: 8 customers
- Month 12: 2,048 customers
- End of year: 4,000+ total customers

**Real Example — Gym Launch Year 1:** Spent $100K, made $10M (100:1 return). Not limited by capital, only by market size and operational capacity.

### The Supplement Origin Story
- Facebook 5X'd lead costs, gym was bleeding cash
- Met college friend selling $80-130K/month in supplements with no gym
- Started selling $200/person in supplements at gym ($100 profit)
- $100 supplement profit offset $100 CAC exactly
- Result: got paid to acquire new customers instead of paying to acquire them
- Moral: making another offer made more money and funded growth

## 4. The Four Types of Offers

Every money model is built from four offer categories, each with a distinct objective:

| Type | Objective | Key Question |
|------|-----------|--------------|
| **Attraction Offers** | Liquidate customer acquisition cost | How do we get customers in the door? |
| **Upsell Offers** | Maximize profit | How do we get them to spend more? |
| **Downsell Offers** | Maximize conversion | What do we offer when they say no? |
| **Continuity Offers** | Stabilize cash flow | How do we keep them paying? |

### How They Work Together
1. **Attraction** → Turn strangers into customers
2. **Upsell** → Get customers to spend more money quickly
3. **Downsell** → Convert people who would have said no into yeses
4. **Continuity** → Keep customers buying on a recurring basis

### The Gym Launch Case Study (Math Comparison)

**Old Way (Low Barrier Offer / LBO):**
- 10 walk-ins → 8 buy $29 trial → 3 convert (35% industry average)
- 30-day gross profit: $600
- Revenue per show-up: $60

**New Way (Money Model):**
- 10 walk-ins → sell to goal: 1 × $2K + majority × $500 + smaller packages
- Upsell meals, supplements ($85 GP/person), continuity, prepayment
- 30-day gross profit: $9,644
- Revenue per show-up: $960

**Result:** 16X more gross profit. Could outspend competitors 25:1 on advertising.

## 5. Money Models & Offer Stacks

### Definition
A **money model** is a series of offers structured to minimize CAC, maximize gross profit, collected in the first 30 days.

### Principles
- Start with one offer, add layers like layers of paint
- Scale with as few offers as humanly possible
- Add more only when needed to enable scaling
- Minimize operational drag

### The Rental Car Counter Example
A single rental car reservation produced 9 sequential upsells: late checkout, insurance upgrade, minimum package, prepaid gas, late return fees, car class upgrade, GPS, satellite radio, and rewards program. Each felt natural and was offered at the right moment.

### Key Insight
The best money models feel like a natural journey, not a sales gauntlet. Each offer solves the next logical problem the customer faces.

## 6. Attraction Offers

Attraction offers bring new customers in the door and ideally liquidate (or exceed) the cost of acquiring them.

### 6a. Win-Your-Money-Back

**How it works:**
1. Set a goal for the customer (e.g., lose 20 pounds in 8 weeks)
2. Customer pays upfront (e.g., $500)
3. If they hit the goal, they get their money back (or store credit)
4. Use before/after results as marketing assets

**Why it works:**
- Creates high accountability
- Before/after proof becomes a marketing asset worth far more than the refund
- Works best in transformation-based businesses

**Variations:**
- Full cash refund on goal completion
- Store credit refund (keeps money in ecosystem)
- Free trial version with refund guarantee

### 6b. Free Giveaways

**Two-Part Structure:**
1. **Grand Prize** — free high-value item, roughly equal to the value of your main offer (if you sell a $40K program, give away a $40K prize)
2. **Participation Trophy** — promotional offer for everyone who doesn't win, sized to **10-30% of your gross margin** (not 10-30% off retail)

**Timeline (3-7 Days):**
1. Announce grand prize, create excitement
2. Daily updates + value content about the prize
3. Show benefits and social proof each day
4. Announce winner publicly
5. Offer everyone else the promotional deal (3-7 day deadline)

**Application Questions to Ask:**
- Why should we pick you?
- Why this program? Why now?
- What are your goals/pain points?
- (Answers provide lead scoring data AND sales conversation material)

**Advanced Tactic — Dual Prizes:**
"If the person you refer wins, you win too." Dramatically increases referrals (2-4X lead volume).

**Rules:**
- No cash giveaways (less original, less margin)
- Grand prize must be genuinely attractive
- If it doesn't work, the grand prize wasn't grand enough

### 6c. Decoy Offers

**How it works:**
1. Advertise a free/cheap "decoy" offer (low barrier)
2. When leads engage, present both the decoy AND a superior premium offer side-by-side
3. 70-80% should choose the premium

**Building the Decoy:**
1. Start with your premium offer
2. Remove features (fewer components, older models, less personalization)
3. Strip down to bare minimum — that's your decoy
4. Advertise ONLY the decoy

**Closing:**
- Present premium first with enthusiasm
- "Which one do you think will get you to your goal faster?"
- If fewer than 70% take premium, offers aren't different enough — increase the contrast

**Sorting Question (if uncomfortable leading with premium):**
- "Are you here for free stuff or lasting results?"
- Most say "lasting results" → permission to present premium first

### 6d. Buy X Get Y Free

**Core power:** "Buy 1, get 2 free" converts better than "66% off" — identical economics, different psychology.

**Key principle — give more free than paid:**
- "Buy 1 get 2" beats "buy 2 get 1"
- "Buy 6 months get 12 months" beats "buy 12 months get 6 months"

**Variations of the same $1,800 offer:**
- Buy 12 months, get 6 free (least compelling)
- Buy 9 months, get 9 free
- Buy 6 months, get 12 free (most compelling)

**Rules:**
- Cap recurring revenue prepayments at 10% of customer base
- Still upsell to prepaid customers (they're hyper-buyers)
- Set up recurring billing after discount period ends
- Spread discount evenly across term (don't front-load then surprise with full price)

### 6e. Pay Less Now / Pay More Later

**Structure:**
- **Pay Later:** $0 down, full price after delivery (e.g., $300 after 3-hour training), cancel if not satisfied
- **Pay Now:** 33-66% discount + bonuses (e.g., $97 + recordings), lock in price

**Why it works:**
- Removes all customer risk (satisfaction guarantee + delayed payment)
- Can advertise as "free" since $0 down
- Gives customers agency in their choice

**Optimization:**
- Too many pick pay-later → increase pay-now discount or add better bonuses
- Too many pick pay-now → likely a product/delivery issue
- Target ~10% cancellation rate for pay-later

### 6f. Free with Consumption

**Formula:** Free consumption → trust building → expensive offer acceptance

**Core insight:** "The best way for someone to know they'll get value from you is to give them value before they pay."

**Time requirements by price point:**
- $20 product: 7-second ad sufficient
- $500 product: likely need phone sales
- $5,000+ product: need significant educational content

**Five-Day Challenge Structure:**
- Day 1: Origin story, perspective shift, how you can help
- Day 2: Address internal insecurity, then external excuse
- Day 3: Mention upsell coming tomorrow
- Day 4: Pitch with stacked offer
- Day 5: Re-pitch with Q&A, case studies, proof

**Three Beliefs You Must Break:**
1. **Circumstances:** "It's not my circumstances, it's my resourcefulness"
2. **Other People:** "I'm responsible for my own results"
3. **Self-Doubt:** "I'm capable of this"

**Belief-Breaking Formula:**
1. "This is what you used to believe"
2. "This is why it's wrong"
3. "This is what's right"
4. "This is why it's right"
5. Proof

**Conversion Benchmarks:**
- Virtual webinars: ~10% of live attendees
- Dinners/in-person: ~10% of total attendees
- Virtual summits: 2-5% of opt-ins

**Content Rule:** Teach concepts, not tactics. Everyone gets value from concepts; only executors get value from tactics.

## 7. Upsell Offers

Upsell offers are made after the initial sale to increase customer lifetime value and profits. They often comprise the majority of profit even with smaller transaction volume.

**The Burger Shop Math:**
- $2 burger with $1.75 COGS = $0.25 GP per sale
- Add fries, soda, supersize via upsells → $1.75 GP per sale
- **8X gross profit from the same transaction**
- Even though burgers sell 10X more than fries, the majority of profit comes from the upsells

### 7a. Classic Upsell

**Core principle:** "You can't have X without Y."

**How it works:**
1. Provide service A for free (or low cost)
2. Upsell service B as a natural, essential accompaniment
3. Frame as required for success

**The No-Sell Sale:** "You don't want anything else, do you?" People conditioned to say "no" to salespeople end up saying "no" to not buying.

**Target close rate: 80%+.** If you're getting 30%, you're not making it feel assumed enough, or it's not clear the two things go together. Benchmark: McDonald's gets ~80% on fries and Coke with burgers.

**Examples:**
- Storage unit → locks, boxes, dollies, insurance
- Gym membership → supplements, nutrition plans
- Physical therapy → orthotics, bands, braces, kinesio tape

### 7b. Anchor Upsell

**Core principle:** Everything is relative. Present the premium option first at 5-10X the price. Customer gasps. Then present the main offer — it feels like a bargain.

**Steps:**
1. Present anchor (premium, 5-10X price)
2. Get the gasp (if no gasp, not anchored high enough)
3. Ask about secondary features ("Do you care about [premium-only feature]?")
4. Present main offer (1/5 to 1/10 price)
5. Customer feels relief, sees a good deal
6. Ask how they want to pay

**Key requirement:** Change ONLY secondary features between anchor and main offer. Primary features (core functionality) stay the same.

**Mathematical impact:** If 1 in 10 buy the 10X premium version, it effectively doubles the average revenue per customer.

### 7c. Menu Upsell

**Four-Step Framework:**

1. **Unsell** — cross out what they don't need ("You're not trying to gain weight, right?"). Builds trust.
2. **Prescribe** — give detailed usage instructions, assuming the purchase ("Take 2 of these in the morning, 1 at night, with food")
3. **Ask Preference** — "Vanilla or chocolate?" (not "Do you want this?"). Both options = purchase.
4. **Card on File** — "Want to use the card ending in 4696?" Removes all friction.

**Pro tactic:** Keep products you'll never sell just to cross them out during the unsell step. It builds credibility.

### 7d. Rollover Upsell

**Core principle:** Credit some or all of a customer's previous purchase toward the next (more expensive) offer.

**Four situations to use it:**

1. **Win-Back (old customers):** Email subject: "I owe you money." Offer credit toward a new, better service.
2. **Rescue (upset customers):** Alternative to refund. "Take your credit toward what I should've sold you originally." Redo instead of refund.
3. **Steal competitors' customers:** Find one-star reviews. "How much did you spend there? I'll credit that toward my service."
4. **Upsell current customers:** Roll lower-tier purchase toward higher-tier offer.

**Pricing rule:** Upsell should be 4-5X the rollover credit (minimum). Rolling $500? Upsell must be $2,000-2,500+.

**Gift Card Hack:** Sell $200 gift cards at 90% off ($20 each). Can only be used for others or larger purchases. Customer pays $20 to refer a friend; you apply $200 credit toward their $2,000 offer. Get paid to acquire referrals.

## 8. Downsell Offers

Downsell offers reduce customer rejection by providing viable alternatives when they say no to the primary offer. A downsell must remove value proportionally to reduce price — never lower price without changing what they get.

### 8a. Payment Plans

**Critical principle:** People reject offers when they cost too much TODAY, not necessarily too much overall.

**Seven-Step Downsell Process:**

1. **Reward prepayment, don't punish installments** — anchor highest price first (payment plan), then offer prepay discount
2. **Third-party financing** — credit cards, financing companies (+35% revenue average)
3. **Split 50/50** — "Half today, half next payday?"
4. **Temperature check** — "1-10, how bad do you want this?" (8+ = continue; below 8 = switch to feature downsells)
5. **Three payments** — one-third now, one-third in 2 weeks, one-third in 4 weeks
6. **Equal payments** — spread evenly over service duration
7. **Free trial** — last resort before losing the sale

**Key optimization:** Schedule billing to paycheck dates (usually biweekly). Gets 3 payments in 30 days vs. 1 if monthly. Far fewer declined payments.

**Biweekly Billing Hack:** Bill every 2 weeks aligned to paycheck dates — ~3 payments in the first 30 days vs. 1 on a monthly cycle. Compresses cash collection and reduces declined-card events. (The "13 cycles/yr = +8.3%" math is a doc extrapolation, not stated in the course.)

**Salesperson compensation:** Pay commission on cash collected TODAY (not promised). Aligns incentives.

### 8b. Free Trials (Trial with Penalty)

**How it works:** Free trial if customer meets terms; charged fee if they don't. Avoids non-consumption.

**Seven-step process:**
1. Offer trials LAST (after they've said no to everything else)
2. Always get credit card upfront
3. Sell staying and paying: "If this gets you results, will you stay long term?"
4. Sell consumption commitment
5. Make check-ins required (initial next to each fee)
6. Determine fees: 3-4 mandatory activities, weighted by importance
7. Total fees should equal full upfront cost

**Fee structure:** Weight by importance (sales consults = $100-150, other items = $25-50). Allow 1-2 makeups.

**Naming:** Call it a "trial" — never use "penalty" language.

### 8c. Feature Downsells

**Core concept:** Lower prices by removing features, forcing customers to weigh savings against value loss.

**Service Quality Dimensions to Adjust:**
1. Response time (5-minute vs. overnight)
2. Time availability (24/7 vs. business hours)
3. Days of week (all days vs. Mon/Wed/Fri)
4. Amount of time per session (60 min vs. 15 min)
5. Location availability (multiple vs. one)
6. Speed of delivery (same day vs. next week)
7. Service ratio (1-on-1 vs. group)
8. Communication method (live vs. chat vs. recorded)
9. Provider qualifications (owner vs. new hire)
10. Live vs. recorded
11. In-person vs. remote
12. DIY vs. done-with-you vs. done-for-you
13. Personalization (generic vs. custom)
14. Guarantee terms (duration, coverage, conditions)

**Process:**
1. Cut something valuable, lower price a LITTLE → triggers re-upsell ("Actually, I want that back")
2. If they say no, put the valuable thing back, remove something else, take a BIG price cut
3. Continue until they buy

**Pro tactic:** Remove the guarantee first — highest uptake for re-upsell.

**Exception to "never lower price without changing what they get":** Change what they GIVE you instead (reviews, testimonials, referrals, social posts) = barter pricing.

## 9. Continuity Offers

Continuity offers create recurring revenue — customers make ongoing payments until they cancel.

### Why Continuity Matters

**Business valuation impact:**
- Transactional $10M revenue, $3M EBITDA: 4X multiple = $12M enterprise value
- Recurring $10M revenue, $3M EBITDA: 8-15X multiple = $24-45M enterprise value
- Recurring businesses worth 2-4X more at sale

**Lifetime Revenue Formula:** `Price / Monthly Churn Rate = Lifetime Revenue`

**Example:** $100/month at 10% churn = $1,000 LTV. Drop churn to 3% → ~$3,333 LTV (3X+ increase from a 7-point churn reduction; Hormozi rounds this to "$3,000" in the course).

**Core metric: Churn is king.** Every 1% decrease in monthly churn = exponential LTV increase.

**"Big Head, Long Tail" Principle:** Upfront payment ($1,000) + ongoing payment ($50/month) creates sunk cost psychology and dramatically lowers churn vs. just $50/month.

### 9a. Continuity Bonus Offers

**Core principle:** Advertise the BONUS, not the membership. The bonus should have more perceived value than the first month's payment.

**Examples:**
- $24,000 worth of courses free if you join $400/month newsletter
- 6-week accelerator ($1,000 value) free with $100/month membership
- Dog toys with pet food subscription

**Standalone vs. Continuity Pricing Data:**
| Price Premium (standalone) | % Taking Continuity |
|---|---|
| 1.33X | 50% |
| 1.66X | 60% |
| 2X | 70% |
| 2.3X | 80% |
| 2.66X | 90% |
| 3X | "almost everybody" |

People pay roughly 33% more to avoid the hassle of continuity/cancellation.

**Status/Title Bonuses:** Give customers tiered status (Silver, Gold, Diamond) unlocked at milestones (90-day, 6-month, 12-month). Cheapest bonus to give, highest perceived value.

**Prepaid Continuity Upsell:** "Buy 5 months, get 1 free." Only 1 in 8 need to take it to raise 30-day profits by 50%.

### 9b. Continuity Discount Offers

**Four ways to apply a time-based discount:**

1. **Front-Loaded:** Free months upfront, then commitment begins (e.g., 3 free + 12 paid = 15-month agreement)
2. **Back-Loaded:** Pay full price, earn free months at the end (better for cash flow)
3. **Spread Over Time:** Distribute discount evenly across all months (e.g., $50/month off for 12 months)
4. **Gift Card/Rebate:** Discount in gift card form — they redeem, gift to a friend, or forget (all outcomes benefit you)

**Lifetime Discount at Churn Point:**
- Set discount to kick in at your known highest churn month
- If 50% churn at month 4, offer 15% lifetime discount starting month 4
- Remind them as they approach: "2 months until your lifetime discount kicks in"

**Getting Two Forms of Payment:**
- Collect card AND ACH (bank account)
- Justify with: "3% processing fee waived if you provide ACH"
- ACH rarely declines → dramatically reduces involuntary churn

**Cancellation Best Practices:**
- Make cancellation EASY (prevents review bombs)
- "Fee or Feedback" approach: waive breakup fee in exchange for exit interview
- Be MORE upset about the problem than they are — they'll try to calm you down
- Have customers initial/sign cancellation clauses so nobody is surprised

### 9c. Waived Fee Offers

**Structure:**
- Month-to-month: $X startup fee + $Y/month (e.g., $5,000 setup + $1,000/month)
- Committed: $0 setup fee + $Y/month, but pay fee if cancel early
- Startup fee = 3-5X monthly rate

**Why it works:**
- Fees get them to START (sign up to avoid the big fee)
- Fees get them to STICK (don't want to pay to leave)
- Sunk cost psychology kicks in

**Creative names for fees:** Initiation, setup, activation, engagement, enrollment, processing, build, commencement

**Cancellation Fee for a Cause:**
- "What cause do you hate? If you cancel early, I donate your setup fee to that cause"
- Dual motivation: don't want to pay AND don't want to fund something they oppose

**Target:** Less than 5% early cancellation rate. Above 5% = product issue, not fee issue.

## 10. Building Your Money Model

### Five Steps

**Step 1: Pick ONE Attraction Offer** — How do you get customers?
- Win-Your-Money-Back
- Free Giveaway
- Decoy Offer
- Buy X Get Y Free
- Pay Less Now / Pay More Later
- Free with Consumption

**Step 2: Pick ONE Upsell Offer** — What do you offer after they buy?
- Classic Upsell
- Menu Upsell
- Anchor Upsell
- Rollover Upsell

**Step 3: Pick ONE Downsell Offer** — What if they say no?
- Payment Plans
- Trial with Penalty
- Feature Downsells

**Step 4: Pick ONE Continuity Offer** — How do you keep them paying?
- Continuity Bonus
- Continuity Discount
- Waived Fee

**Step 5: Stack Them** — Combine into a single customer journey.

### Implementation Rules

1. **Perfect one offer at a time** — don't launch all four simultaneously (it will break your business)
2. **Start simple** — use the fewest pieces possible
3. **Start low-price** — get people saying yes, then raise over time
4. **Use payment plans to test pricing** — reveals what the market will sustain
5. **Simple scales, fancy fails** — operational complexity kills growth
6. **Affiliate products can fill gaps** — use other people's products in your model if needed
7. **Turn any offer into continuity** — add a renewal clause to any attraction offer

### Implementation Order

1. Get customers reliably (attraction)
2. Make sure they pay for themselves (upsells + downsells)
3. Make sure they pay for OTHER customers (profit > acquisition cost)
4. Maximize long-term value (continuity)
5. Scale

### Two Tiers of Money Models

**Good Money Model:** Makes more profit from one customer than it costs to get AND service them in the first 30 days. This is the entry threshold for sustainability.

**$100M Money Model:** Makes more profit from one customer than it costs to get AND service MANY customers in the first 30 days. This removes cash as the limiter to scaling and enables virtually infinite growth.
